# RCT Ecosystem – December 2025 **Simulated Benchmark** (Executive Summary)

> **Important:** This document summarises a **simulated benchmark** of the RCT Ecosystem.
> All numbers are generated under controlled internal assumptions and **do not**
> represent an independent third‑party audit or real‑world user traffic yet.

---

## 1. What is RCT? (Intent‑Driven AI & Knowledge OS)

**RCT (Reverse Component Thinking)** is an **intent‑driven AI & knowledge operating system**.

Instead of being “just another chatbot” or “agent framework”, RCT positions itself as:

- A **Kernel** that manages human **Intent** as a first‑class object
- A **Knowledge Layer** (Vault‑1068, GraphRAG, RCTDB) that the kernel can read/write
- A library of **Mini Kernels / Mini ArtentAI** for specific use‑cases
- A set of **developer surfaces** (CLI / API / IDE prompts) for building products on top

In short: **Foundation LLMs are the muscles; RCT is the operating system that coordinates them
around human intent and long‑term knowledge.**

This benchmark asks: *“If we treat RCT as an Intent‑Driven AI OS, how strong, stable and
cost‑efficient is it under realistic simulated conditions?”*

---

## 2. What did we test? (7 benchmark phases)

The December 2025 simulated benchmark runs across **7 phases** over **31 days**:

1. **Phase 0 – Baseline & Environment**
   - Define reference hardware on Hetzner (3 nodes)
   - Fix model mix, tool set, and kernel configuration
2. **Phase 1 – Performance & Latency**
   - Load tests across multiple concurrency levels
   - Measure p50 / p95 latency and error rates
3. **Phase 2 – Endurance & Reliability**
   - 72‑hour continuous run at fixed concurrent load
   - Track uptime, memory behaviour, error patterns
4. **Phase 3 – Accuracy & Reasoning (GAIA‑style tasks)**
   - Multi‑level evaluation on 1,000+ complex tasks
   - Focus on structured reasoning, planning, coding, research
5. **Phase 4 – Hallucination & Safety**
   - Stress‑test hallucination detection on synthetic false statements
   - Measure precision/recall of refusal vs correction
6. **Phase 5 – Knowledge / Search Performance**
   - Compare RCT’s GraphRAG + Qdrant stack vs alternative search stacks
   - Focus on top‑k accuracy and latency
7. **Phase 6 – Cost & Efficiency**
   - Estimate cost per 1M requests under the same load profile
   - Compare against typical SaaS / API‑only setups

All phases are simulated using internal scripts and datasets. No real end users are involved
in this round.

---

## 3. Key numbers (high‑level)

*(Rounded for public summary; see full PDF report for complete tables.)*

- **Stability & Reliability**
  - 72‑hour endurance run: uptime ~**99.98%**, no data loss observed
  - Error rate during endurance: ≈ **0.002%** (mostly transient network/API issues)
- **Performance**
  - Under moderate load (hundreds of concurrent users), median latency in the
    **hundreds of milliseconds**, with graceful degradation under stress
  - System continues to respond under several thousand concurrent sessions before
    latency crosses multi‑second range
- **Reasoning / GAIA‑style tasks**
  - Internal GAIA‑style evaluation of 1,000 tasks yields an aggregated score of
    **≈ 86–87 points**, substantially above typical public baselines (≈ 70s) under
    our test configuration.
- **Knowledge & Search**
  - Semantic search (GraphRAG + vector DB) achieves **high top‑1 and near‑perfect top‑5**
    answer retrieval on the internal corpus used in testing.
- **Cost**
  - Under the simulated traffic profile, the estimated cost is roughly
    **$0.004–0.005 per request**, which corresponds to an approximate
    **13–30× cost efficiency gain** versus several reference SaaS/API setups
    we modelled.

These numbers should be treated as **internal engineering signals**, not as external
marketing claims or SLAs.

## 4. Limitations (what this benchmark is *not*)

This simulation has **important limitations**:

1. **No real user traffic yet**
   - All traffic is generated by scripts under controlled conditions.
   - User behaviour in production (stop/start, think time, burst patterns) may differ.
2. **Single‑operator, single‑tenant environment**
   - The test environment is operated and instrumented by the RCT team only.
   - Multi‑tenant, multi‑team scenarios may reveal new bottlenecks.
3. **Internal metrics, not independent audit**
   - Results have not yet been verified by an external lab or third‑party auditor.
   - Comparative baselines (e.g. other frameworks) are best‑effort internal estimates.
4. **Scope limited to current RCT configuration**
   - Different model mixes, knowledge sizes, or tool sets may change behaviour.
   - Kernel and Mini Kernel designs will continue to evolve.

Because of these constraints, the numbers should be read as:
> “RCT appears **production‑ready in theory** and highly promising under internal
> simulations, but still needs **real traffic + external validation**.”

## 5. Future work

The next steps for the RCT benchmark programme are:

1. **Real‑world pilot deployments**
   - Run the same instrumentation on small production pilots (RCTLabs, ArtentAI,
     SignedAI) with real users.
2. **External / third‑party evaluation**
   - Invite independent researchers or partner companies to reproduce the benchmark
     and publish their findings.
3. **Expanded GAIA & task coverage**
   - Increase task diversity (multi‑modal, longer‑horizon planning, team workflows).
4. **Kernel & Mini Kernel iterations**
   - Use benchmark results to refine the Intent‑Driven Kernel, Mini Kernel templates,
     and knowledge routing strategies.
5. **Public tooling & open artefacts**
   - Release public benchmark scripts, configs and sample data so others can
     reuse the methodology for their own systems.
