---  ## 2.1 High-Level View: Three Domains, One Foundation  At the top level, the RCT ecosystem presents itself as **three external-facing domains** built on a shared architectural foundation:  1. **RCTLabs.co — The Laboratory & Public Front Door**  2. **ArtentAI — The Capability & Workflow Platform**  3. **SignedAI — The Verification & Trust Layer**  Internally, all three domains share the same backbone:  - The **FDIA equation** as a design principle:  \[  F = (D^I) \times A  \]  where **A = Architect**, not Action.  - The **JITNA intent language** as the internal protocol for every request  (`I, D, Δ, A, R, M`). - The **Vault-1068 + RCTDB** as shared memory and knowledge backbone. - A family of **36 algorithms across 9 tiers** as the meta-architecture of reasoning,  orchestration and verification. - A containerized infrastructure (22 containers now, 33 in the future) to run  multi-LLM and tool-based workflows.  From the outside, the ecosystem looks like three different “products”. From the inside, it is **one coherent architecture** governed by FDIA, JITNA and the Architect Model.  ### 2.1.1 RCTLabs.co — Laboratory & Public Front Door  RCTLabs.co is the **entry point** for users, partners, and the public.  It hosts:  - the **Floating AI Assistant**  - a demo surface for the architecture,  - a real utility for exploring the vault, experiments, and ecosystem. - documentation, research briefs, and selected benchmark results. - launch pages, pricing, and onboarding flows.  **Role relative to the 8 AI problems (Chapter 1):**  - Makes the **problem space legible**: context window crisis, hallucination, fragile intent.


<!-- Page 3 of 12 -->


- Exposes RCT’s responses in an accessible way (without revealing all internal details). - Provides a controlled environment where people can **experience FDIA/JITNA behavior**  through the Floating Assistant before touching deeper platforms.  **FDIA/JITNA view:**  - Dominant roles:  - FDIA: F (user-facing outcomes), I (why the user came), A (how the Architect chooses to present capabilities).  - JITNA: I (intent discovery), D (surface-level data), R (user feedback), M (basic preference memory).  ---  ### 2.1.2 ArtentAI — Capability & Workflow Platform  ArtentAI is the **engine room** of the RCT ecosystem.  It focuses on:  - **Capability libraries**  - reusable functions, flows, and “skills” built on top of the 36 algorithms. - **Workflow builders & no-code tools**  - letting architects and advanced users assemble workflows without low-level coding. - **UI-from-intent systems**  - deriving interfaces directly from JITNA-coded intents. - **Internal tools for orchestrating agents and experiments**  - controlling which models, tools, and algorithms are used for which tasks.  This is where **FDIA + JITNA + Vault + RCTDB** are surfaced as *usable building blocks*.  **Role relative to the 8 AI problems:**  - Addresses **intent fragility (Problem 3)** by encoding intent as JITNA, not ad-hoc prompts. - Addresses **static architectures (Problem 5)** by allowing dynamic, tier-aware workflows. - Addresses **tool fragmentation (Problem 6)** by embedding tools into a shared language and memory stack.  **FDIA/JITNA view:**  - FDIA:  - I (precise, long-lived intents), D (rich access to vault & RCTDB),  - A (architected workflows), F (project-level outcomes). - JITNA:  - I, Δ, A are heavily manipulated;  - R and M become crucial for long-term workflows and experiments.  ---

 ### 2.1.3 SignedAI — Verification & Trust Layer  SignedAI is the **verification and trust layer** of the ecosystem.  Its responsibilities:  - Check, verify, and “sign” AI outputs. - Maintain evaluations, scores, and risk labels. - Provide a **long-term record** of what was decided, why, and under which constraints. - Eventually stand as a **verification infrastructure** that could serve multiple ecosystems,  not only RCT.  **Role relative to the 8 AI problems:**  - Addresses **opaque reasoning (Problem 4)** and **evaluation mismatch (Problem 7)**.  - Provides systematic **human-in-the-loop oversight (Problem 8)**:  - some decisions require an Architect or reviewer to sign off,  - every signed decision is recorded in RCTDB with its JITNA state.  **FDIA/JITNA view:**  - FDIA:  - Strong emphasis on A (Architect governance) and F (acceptable future states).  - JITNA:  - Heavy use of R (Reflection/Review) and M (what to store and replay later).  ---  ## 2.2 Architectural Layers  We can understand the ecosystem in terms of **five layers**:  1. **Foundational Principles Layer** — FDIA, JITNA, Architect Model.  2. **Knowledge & Memory Layer** — Vault-1068, RCTDB, manifests, genomes.  3. **Algorithm & Workflow Layer** — 36 algorithms, RCT-7, routing, verification.  4. **Infrastructure Layer** — containers, MCP toolkit, external models, observability.  5. **Product & UX Layer** — RCTLabs, ArtentAI, SignedAI, Floating Assistant, portals.  ### 2.2.1 Foundational Principles Layer  This is the layer codified in **Phase 0**:  - **FDIA — Future Design Intelligence Algorithm**  \[  F = (D^I) \times A  \]  - F = Future Outcome (how well the system matches the desired future trajectory).  - D = Data (coverage, cleanliness, relevance).  - I = Intent (clarity and correctness of the goal).  - A = Architect (human-in-the-loop design & governance).  - **JITNA — Just-In-Time Narrative Assembly**  - `I, D, Δ, A, R, M` as the minimal unit of intent and system state.  - Every non-trivial interaction is represented as a JITNA packet.  - **Architect Model**  - Encodes human values, goals, and constraints into the system.  - Distinguishes **Architect** from Algorithms and Actions.  - Embeds codices and constitutions as a structural layer.  This layer answers:  - *“How should we think about AI systems at all?”*  - *“What does ‘good’ look like in terms of intent, architecture, and future outcomes?”*  Every other layer exists to **implement and respect** these principles.  ---  ### 2.2.2 Knowledge & Memory Layer: Vault-1068 & RCTDB  The second layer turns FDIA/JITNA into concrete memory structures.  - **Vault-1068**  - 1,068 curated files (Markdown, PDFs, JSON, CSV, code, diagrams, etc.).  - Each file has YAML front-matter aligned with **MANIFEST_1068.json**.  - Tagged with FDIA + JITNA roles, for example:  ```yaml  jitna_role: ["D", "Delta"]

<!-- Page 6 of 12 -->


  fdia_role: ["D"]  ```  - Acts as the **canonical knowledge base** for:  - RCT system design and architecture,  - experiments and benchmarks,  - algorithms and workflows,  - UX designs and roadmaps,  - pricing and launch plans.  - **RCTDB**  - A database for:  - experiment results (thousands of runs, hundreds of scenarios),  - long-term memory of JITNA packets and outcomes,  - deltas: what changed, when, and why,  - meta-information on system evolution.  - Supports queries such as:  - “Which experiments led us to adopt Algorithm X in Tier 4?”  - “What was the system’s state at date Y and what did FDIA look like then?”  - “How did improving I or A affect F across multiple runs?”  **FDIA/JITNA mapping at this layer:**  - **D** (Data) → core of the vault: research, specs, experiments, UX docs.  - **I** (Intent) → design documents, Architect’s Genome, long-term roadmaps.  - **Δ** (Delta) → comparison docs, change logs, before/after analyses.  - **A** (Architecture) → system design, container maps, algorithm specs.  - **R & M** → reflection reports, “lessons learned”, RCTDB logs and schemas.  The goal is:  > Any agent in the ecosystem should be able to ask a question in JITNA,  > retrieve the right slices of Vault-1068/RCTDB, and reason over them  > in a reproducible and auditable way.  ---  ### 2.2.3 Algorithm & Workflow Layer: 36 Algorithms, 9 Tiers  Above memory sits the **Algorithm & Workflow Layer** — the “thinking machinery” of RCT.  - **9 Tiers** form a vertical stack, roughly:  1. Input & intent parsing  2. Retrieval & context building  3. Drafting & candidate generation

  4. Self-critique & refinement  5. Verification & testing  6. Synthesis & packaging  7. Orchestration & routing  8. Long-term learning & delta integration  9. Meta-governance & Architect loops  - **36 Algorithms** are distributed across these tiers:  - some are single-LLM,  - others are multi-LLM or agent-based,  - some operate on text, others on code, workflows, or diagrams.  [oai_citation:2‡Chapter02_Ecosystem_Architecture_Overview.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000bee07206896f22e8b5cd2367)  Each algorithm has:  - a clear **FDIA role** (e.g. “boosts I”, “compresses D”, “implements A”, “protects F”),  - and a **JITNA role** (e.g. transforms Δ, performs R, writes to M).  **Examples (conceptual):**  - **Tier 1 — Intent Parser**  - Converts user text (from RCTLabs, ArtentAI, or SignedAI) into a JITNA packet.  - FDIA: increases I by clarifying goals and constraints.  - **Tier 2–3 — Delta Planner**  - Looks at Δ to decide how many steps and which algorithms are needed.  - FDIA: shapes A by choosing architecture patterns and routes.  - **Tier 5 — Verifier / SignedAI-related**  - Cross-checks outputs against constraints, tests, and prior decisions.  - FDIA: protects F from silent degradation; modulates A by enforcing codices.  Workflows in RCT are **compositions** of these algorithms, chosen dynamically based on:  - the JITNA state of the request (I, D, Δ, risk level),  - system constraints (budget, latency),  - Architect’s policies embedded in codices.  ---  ### 2.2.4 Infrastructure Layer: Containers, MCP Toolkit, Multi-LLM  The **Infrastructure Layer** turns algorithms into running services.  #### 2.2.4.1 22 Containers (Current Phase)

<!-- Page 8 of 12 -->


In the current phase, the RCT ecosystem is designed around **22 core containers**, including:  - **Gateway & API Layer**  - Entry points for RCTLabs, ArtentAI, SignedAI, and internal tools.  - **Orchestrator / Router**  - Evaluates JITNA packets and chooses which algorithms/models to call.  - **Model Workers**  - Multiple LLM backends with different strengths (reasoning, speed, coding, etc.).  - **Retrieval & GraphRAG Services**  - Indexes and graph-based retrieval over Vault-1068 and RCTDB.  - **Experiment Runner**  - Executes and logs structured tests, writing into RCTDB.  - **Verification Services (SignedAI)**  - Scoring, consistency checks, multi-model cross-checking.  - **Observability & Logging**  - Metrics, traces, audit trails, cost tracking.  These 22 containers are chosen to:  - keep the system **buildable and operable by a single architect/team**,  - already express the full **pattern** needed for later scaling to 33 containers.  #### 2.2.4.2 33 Containers (Future Vision)  The **33-container design** extends the 22 by:  - splitting some roles into more granular services, e.g.:  - domain-specific verifiers,  - dedicated simulation environments,  - specialized ingestion pipelines. - adding dedicated containers for:  - pricing & billing logic,  - partner integrations,  - large-scale experiments and A/B frameworks.  The rule is:  - The **conceptual architecture remains the same** (FDIA/JITNA, 36 algorithms, 9 tiers).  - Containers are refined to improve **scalability, resilience, and separation of concerns**  without redesigning the model.

#### 2.2.4.3 MCP Toolkit & External Tools  The ecosystem is not closed:  - Uses an **MCP-style toolkit** approach:  - treat external tools (HTTP APIs, DBs, services) as first-class agents.  - This lets RCT:  - call external knowledge bases,  - integrate billing and operational systems,  - interact with third-party AI and infra.  FDIA/JITNA guide these integrations:  - Tools that primarily provide **data** → D-heavy roles.  - Tools that trigger **actions in the world** operate under Architect supervision,  with their behavior modeled as part of F and constrained by A.  ---  ### 2.2.5 Product & UX Layer: How Humans See the System  At the top, the **Product & UX layer** is how humans experience everything:  - **RCTLabs**  - Public site, research briefs, documentation, and pricing.  - Home of the **Floating AI Assistant**, which:  - uses JITNA under the hood,  - can explain FDIA/JITNA/Architect in human terms,  - guides users into deeper platforms.  - **ArtentAI**  - Capability dashboards, workspace UIs, intent-to-workflow builders.  - Provides “from intent to UI” tools where user goals are turned into concrete screens,  flows, and experiments.  - **SignedAI**  - Interfaces for reviewing, scoring, and signing outputs.  - Dashboards showing:  - verification status,  - risk levels,  - historical decision traces.  At this layer, JITNA is visible as:  - structured forms and sliders (e.g. “prefer speed vs safety”),
