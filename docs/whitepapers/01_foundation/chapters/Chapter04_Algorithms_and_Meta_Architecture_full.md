 ## 4.1 Why an Algorithm Stack at All?  In principle, one could imagine “a single powerful LLM with a huge context” solving all tasks.  RCT takes the opposite view:  - The **problem space** (8 AI problems + context window crisis) is multi-dimensional.  - Different tasks need different **inductive biases, tools, and safety regimes**.  - The system must be **observable, improvable, and governable** over years.  Therefore RCT adopts an **algorithm stack** with these goals:  1. **Composability**  - Each tier is a *role*, not a fixed model.  - Algorithms inside a tier can be swapped or upgraded without redesigning everything.  2. **FDIA / JITNA Alignment**  - Every tier carries explicit FDIA roles (D, I, A, F)  - and JITNA roles (I, D, Δ, A, R, M).  - This ensures that system behavior can be analyzed and improved in the same language  used in Phase 0.  3. **Multi-LLM, Multi-Tool**  - Different tiers can call different models, tools, and MCP servers.  - RCTDB and Vault-1068 serve as the shared grounding for all of them.  [oai_citation:2‡Chapter04_Algorithms_and_Meta_Architecture.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_0000000010507206a91b05f4004a54eb)  4. **Traceability & Proof**  - Each step creates artifacts that can be:  - recorded in RCTDB,  - linked back to Vault-1068 documents and experiments,  - verified by SignedAI or other proof mechanisms (Chapter 5).  The rest of this chapter moves from a high-level view of the tiers to the detailed roles they play.  ---  ## 4.2 Overview of the 9 Tiers  The 36 algorithms are grouped into **9 conceptual tiers**:  1. **Tier 1 — Input & Intent Parsing**  2. **Tier 2 — Retrieval & Context Building**  3. **Tier 3 — Planning & Decomposition**  4. **Tier 4 — Draft Generation**  5. **Tier 5 — Self-Critique & Verification**

6. **Tier 6 — Synthesis & Packaging**  7. **Tier 7 — Orchestration & Routing**  8. **Tier 8 — Learning & Memory Integration**  9. **Tier 9 — Meta-Governance & Architect Loops**  [oai_citation:3‡Chapter04_Algorithms_and_Meta_Architecture.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_0000000010507206a91b05f4004a54eb)  Each tier holds **3–5 concrete algorithms** in the v13 spec (e.g., intent parsers, planners, verifiers, meta-evaluators), but in this chapter we stay at **tier level** and emphasize:  - what each tier is responsible for,  - which FDIA / JITNA roles it emphasizes,  - how it interacts with Vault-1068 and RCTDB,  - how it serves the 3 product domains (RCTLabs, ArtentAI, SignedAI).  ---  ## 4.3 FDIA & JITNA Roles per Tier  We first summarize the mapping between tiers and FDIA/JITNA roles.  | Tier | High-Level Function  | FDIA Emphasis  | JITNA Emphasis  | |------|--------------------------------------------------|--------------------|------------------| | 1  | Interpret input & identify intent  | I, A (pattern)  | I  | | 2  | Retrieve and structure relevant knowledge  | D  | D, M  | | 3  | Decide plan & delta (how to change the world)  | I, A  | Δ, A  | | 4  | Produce first-pass outputs  | D, A → candidate F | A  | | 5  | Test, critique, and verify  | F (quality, risk)  | R, Δ  | | 6  | Package results for humans/systems  | F  | I, M  | | 7  | Route requests across models/tools/tiers  | A (meta)  | A  | | 8  | Fold experiences back into memory and patterns  | D, F  | M, R  | | 9  | Govern long-term goals, codex, and constraints  | I, A, F  | I, R, M  |  - Vault-1068 anchors **static knowledge** for almost all tiers.  - RCTDB anchors **dynamic memory, deltas, and proof** especially for Tiers 2, 5, 8, 9. **Representative algorithm families**  - **Natural Intent Parser**  - Detects domain, task type, risk class, urgency.  - Uses Vault-1068 intent definition docs and Architect codex.  - **JITNA Compiler**  - Encodes the request into JITNA:  - `I` = explicit/implicit intent,  - initial view of `D` = current information,  - provisional `Δ` = size and direction of desired change,  - preliminary `A/R/M` fields when known.  - **Constraint & Policy Injector**  - Pulls relevant safety rules and preferences from:  - Vault-1068 (codex, policy docs),  - RCTDB (user/project profiles, historical constraints).  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: strongly **I** (define problem) and early **A** (choose architectural pattern).  - JITNA: chiefly **I**, with seeded **D** and **Δ**.  **Links to other chapters**  - Connects Phase 0 (FDIA/JITNA) to the rest of the stack.  - Direct response to **Problem 3: Intent Fragility** (Chapter 1).  ---  ### 4.4.2 Tier 2 — Retrieval & Context Building  **Purpose**  Collect the **right pieces of memory** so that later tiers are not relying on hallucination or partial knowledge.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **Hybrid GraphRAG Retriever**  - Uses vector similarity, symbolic links (Wiki-like), tags, and FDIA/JITNA metadata.  - Traverses Vault-1068 according to:  - domain,  - FDIA/JITNA roles,  - related_docs/graph from the manifest.  - **Context Compressor & Reshaper**  - Compresses or restructures long docs into:  - summaries aligned with the request,

- snippets tagged by FDIA/JITNA roles,  - graph nodes that can be recombined.  - **Conflict & Staleness Detector**  - Detects conflicts:  - old vs new specs,  - multiple experiments with opposing results,  - superseded design decisions.  - Highlights them rather than silently merging.  - **Context Bundle Assembler**  - Produces a structured **context bundle**:  - prioritized chunks,  - explicit “known unknowns” (gaps),  - pointers back to their sources in Vault-1068 and RCTDB.  [oai_citation:5‡Chapter04_Algorithms_and_Meta_Architecture.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_0000000010507206a91b05f4004a54eb)  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: predominantly **D**.  - JITNA: **D** and **M** (reading long-term memory).  **Addressing Context Window Crisis**  - Instead of trying to “feed everything into the model”,  Tier 2 uses **structured retrieval + compression** so that the *most relevant* information  can be fit into context, with traceable pointers.  ---  ### 4.4.3 Tier 3 — Planning & Decomposition  **Purpose**  Translate “what the user wants” into **how the system will proceed**.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **Delta Planner**  - Uses `Δ` from JITNA and patterns from Vault-1068 to assess:  - complexity,  - risk,  - required depth of planning (single step vs multi-step plan).  - **Task Decomposer**  - Breaks a goal into:  - sub-intents (`I₁, I₂, …`),  - sub-deltas (`Δ₁, Δ₂, …`),


<!-- Page 6 of 13 -->


- dependencies and ordering.  - **Strategy Selector**  - Chooses strategies such as:  - fast heuristic vs fully verified path,  - single-LLM vs mixed LLMs,  - interactive vs batch mode.  - Uses RCTDB outcomes to bias toward historically effective strategies.  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: refine **I**, choose **A**.  - JITNA: emphasize **Δ** (change) and **A** (action/algorithm strategy).  **Bridge**  - This is where **FDIA equation** becomes operational:  “Given our current D and I, what A should we apply to steer F?”  ---  ### 4.4.4 Tier 4 — Draft Generation  **Purpose**  Produce **first-pass artifacts**: text, code, configs, structured plans.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **FDIA-Guided Generator**  - Uses:  - Tier 2 context bundle (D),  - Tier 3 plan (Δ, A),  - FDIA weights to decide how to balance:  - reuse vs innovation,  - speed vs quality.  - **Structured Code/Doc Generator**  - Creates:  - `.md`, `.ts`, `.json`, `.yaml` files,  - directory structures for repos,  - step-by-step procedures.  - Uses style & schema definitions from Vault-1068.  - **Multi-View Generator**  - Outputs:  - main candidate,  - alternative variant (or high vs low risk versions),  - explicit TODO markers for missing data or decisions.

**FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: primarily **A** (execution), using **D** to produce a candidate **F**.  - JITNA: **A** with residual **Δ** to be tightened in Tier 5.  **Important property**  - Drafts are **never final**; they are *inputs* to Tier 5 verification and Tier 6 packaging.  ---  ### 4.4.5 Tier 5 — Self-Critique & Verification  **Purpose**  Ensure that artifacts are **safe, consistent, and aligned** before they are accepted.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **Consistency Checker**  - Compares drafts against:  - specs in Vault-1068 (algorithms, infra, UX, pricing),  - Architect codex,  - constraints defined by FDIA/JITNA roles.  - **Test Runner / Scenario Simulator**  - For code/workflows:  - runs structured tests (from Vault and RCTDB),  - simulates scenarios and edge cases,  - records results into RCTDB.  - **SignedAI Verifier (or equivalent verification tier)**  - Assigns:  - scores for correctness, completeness, safety,  - risk labels (green / yellow / red),  - recommended next actions (accept / revise / block).  - **Reflective Critique Engine**  - Produces:  - textual critique,  - identified weaknesses,  - suggestions for further experiments,  - confidence levels. - FDIA: oriented toward **F** (do we want this future outcome?).  - JITNA: **R** (reflection) and **Δ** (residual issues to fix).  **Link to Chapter 5**  - Tier 5 is the “local proof layer” inside each run.  - Chapter 5 zooms out to the full experiment/benchmark infrastructure.  ---  ### 4.4.6 Tier 6 — Synthesis & Packaging  **Purpose**  Transform internal work into **final, consumable outputs** for humans or systems.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **Multi-View Synthesizer**  - Blends:  - main content,  - explanation of reasoning and trade-offs,  - references back to Vault-1068 and RCTDB,  - explicit limitations or TODOs.  - **Audience-Aware Rewriter**  - Adapts content for:  - expert vs non-expert audiences,  - Thai vs English,  - internal vs external consumption.  - Uses style guides and glossary in Vault-1068.  - **Artifact Builder**  - Assembles:  - file trees,  - repo bundles,  - config packages,  - deployable scripts.  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: heavily **F** — define the future state that the world will see.  - JITNA: transforms internal **I/D/Δ/A/R/M** into final human-readable **I/M** (intent + memory anchors).  ---  ### 4.4.7 Tier 7 — Orchestration & Routing

**Purpose**  Make **global choices** about which tiers, algorithms, models, and tools to use, per request.  **Representative algorithm families**  - **Routing Engine**  - Takes as input:  - JITNA packet,  - FDIA roles,  - system constraints (latency, budget, risk class),  - Architect codex and policies.  - Decides:  - which tiers are required,  - whether to use “light” or “heavy” algorithms,  - which LLM/tool configuration to call.  - **Cost/Latency Balancer**  - Consults:  - pricing data from Vault-1068,  - usage stats from RCTDB.  - Chooses trade-offs (e.g., “fast preview” vs “slow verified”).  - **Fallback & Halting Manager**  - Handles:  - partial failures,  - degraded modes,  - safe halting when constraints or policies cannot be satisfied.  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: strong **A** as meta-architecture.  - JITNA: **A** for “action policy at ecosystem level”.  **Relation to the 3 Domains**  - RCTLabs: routes demo scenarios and Floating Assistant conversations.  - ArtentAI: routes internal capability/workflow generation runs.  - SignedAI: routes verification and audit flows.  ---  ### 4.4.8 Tier 8 — Learning & Memory Integration  **Purpose**  Turn episodic runs into **long-term improvement** of the ecosystem.  **Representative algorithm families**


<!-- Page 10 of 13 -->


- **Experience Summarizer**  - Converts:  - traces, logs, verification results  into:  - new reflection documents in Vault-1068,  - structured records in RCTDB (`experiments`, `runs`).  - **Delta Integrator**  - Identifies:  - new patterns,  - failure modes,  - improved workflows or architectures.  - Proposes updates to:  - FDIA/JITNA patterns,  - algorithm specs,  - codex entries.  - **Memory Curator**  - Decides:  - what to keep as long-term memory,  - what to compress,  - what to retire to avoid noise.  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: **D** (new data) and **F** (trajectory of improvement).  - JITNA: **M** (memory) and **R** (reflection).  **Interaction with Vault-1068 & RCTDB**  - Vault-1068 gets new or revised docs, tagged with updated FDIA/JITNA roles.  - RCTDB records:  - experiment runs,  - deltas,  - derived metrics.  This tier ensures RCT is **self-evolving under governance**, not static.  ---  ### 4.4.9 Tier 9 — Meta-Governance & Architect Loops  **Purpose**  Ensure the whole ecosystem evolves under the **Architect’s principles** and long-term vision.  **Representative algorithm families**


<!-- Page 11 of 13 -->


- **Codex Evaluator**  - Checks alignment between:  - observed behavior (from RCTDB),  - Architect codex and genomes (in Vault-1068).  - **Goal & Roadmap Aligner**  - Evaluates whether:  - experiments and product work advance the stated roadmap,  - resources are spent on the right capabilities,  - the system drifts into unsafe or low-value zones.  - **Meta-Experiment Designer**  - Proposes:  - new test suites and benchmarks,  - stress tests for new models/tools,  - evaluation schemes for future research questions.  Many Tier 9 processes are explicitly **human-in-the-loop**:  - The Architect reviews, edits, or approves outputs.  - Certain decisions cannot be fully automated by design.  **FDIA / JITNA roles**  - FDIA: system-level **I, A, F**.  - JITNA: **I, R, M** at ecosystem scale.  **Bridge to Phase 0**  - Tier 9 operationalizes the idea that **A = Architect** in FDIA:  - not just algorithmic action,  - but an explicit role for human authority, codex, and governance.  ---  ## 4.5 How a Request Flows Through the Stack  A typical RCTLabs / ArtentAI / SignedAI request flows like this:  1. **Tier 1** — Parse input → construct JITNA packet.  2. **Tier 2** — Retrieve + compress relevant knowledge from Vault-1068 & RCTDB.  3. **Tier 3** — Plan path and Δ (delta) through the system.  4. **Tier 4** — Generate first-pass artifacts.  5. **Tier 5** — Critique, test, verify; record findings in RCTDB.  6. **Tier 6** — Package outputs for user/system.  7. **Tier 7** — Orchestrate branches, retries, and model/tool choices.  8. **Tier 8** — Integrate lessons into memory and patterns.

9. **Tier 9** — Periodically evaluate long-term alignment and propose research/tests.  [oai_citation:7‡Chapter04_Algorithms_and_Meta_Architecture.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_0000000010507206a91b05f4004a54eb)  Not every path uses all tiers:  - Simple, low-risk tasks: Tiers 1–4 and 6 may be sufficient.  - High-impact tasks: full 1–9 loop with SignedAI and strong verification.  - Internal research: Tiers 2–5–8 heavily used, with detailed logging.  ---  ## 4.6 Relationship to Vault-1068 & RCTDB  Across all tiers:  - **Vault-1068**  - hosts conceptual specs, UX flows, codex, algorithms, whitepaper chapters, and historical decisions.  - gives the stack a **shared “world-model”** in document form.  - **RCTDB**  - hosts experiment definitions, runs, deltas, memory profiles, and Architect decisions.  - gives the stack a **temporal memory** of how the ecosystem behaves over time.  Together they ensure:  > Algorithms are not just “LLMs with a big prompt”,  > but components in an **architected, verifiable system** that remembers its past,  > understands its present intent, and can adjust its future path.  ---  ## 4.7 Summary and Bridge to Chapter 5  In this chapter we have:  1.
