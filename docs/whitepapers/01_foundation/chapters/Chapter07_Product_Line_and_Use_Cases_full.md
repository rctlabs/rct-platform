---  # Chapter 7 — Product Line and Use Cases  ### RCTLabs, ArtentAI, and SignedAI on Top of the RCT Ecosystem  > This chapter explains how the architectural foundation of RCT becomes  > **real products that people can touch**: RCTLabs, ArtentAI, and SignedAI.  > It also shows how these products expose the ecosystem safely to the public,  > while keeping the core algorithms, vault, and experiments under control.  [oai_citation:1‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  Where previous chapters described:  - **Chapter 1** — The 8 AI problems and the context window crisis.  - **Chapter 2** — The three-domain ecosystem architecture.  - **Chapter 3** — Vault-1068 and RCTDB as knowledge and memory backbone.  - **Chapter 4** — The 9 tiers and 36 algorithms as the reasoning core.  - **Chapter 5** — The proof layer: 520 test cases and 15,000+ experiments.  - **Chapter 6** — The 22→33 container infrastructure and MCP toolkit.  This chapter answers a different question:  > *“If all of that is the engine room, what do real users actually see?”*  The answer is the **Product Layer**:  - **RCTLabs** — front door & lab.  - **ArtentAI** — capability & workflow platform.  - **SignedAI** — verification & trust platform.  [oai_citation:2‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  ---  ## 7.1 Product Layer Overview  From the outside, the RCT ecosystem appears as **three primary domains**:  [oai_citation:3‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  1. **RCTLabs.co — The Front Door & Lab**  - Public-facing site and brand.  - Hosts the **Floating AI Assistant** as the main “demo surface”.  - Explains the architecture, story, and pricing at a high level.  2. **ArtentAI — Capability & Workflow Platform (Platform 1)**  - Internal/expert-facing environment.


<!-- Page 3 of 13 -->


- Focus on **capability libraries**, workflows, and project-scale work.  - Where Architect-level users orchestrate multi-step, multi-model flows.  3. **SignedAI — Verification & Trust Platform (Platform 2)**  - Focus on **verification, scoring, and assurance**.  - Exposes concepts from Chapter 5: Proof Layer, verification tiers.  - Used to sign off on outputs, experiments, or critical decisions.  All three products share the same:  - **foundation** — FDIA, JITNA, Vault-1068, RCTDB, 9 tiers, 36 algorithms,  - **infrastructure** — 22→33 container design, MCP toolkit, observability,  - **governance** — Architect codex, meta-governance (Tier 9).  [oai_citation:4‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  But they surface **different slices** of that foundation, targeted at different users and use cases:  - RCTLabs: *first contact and narrative*.  - ArtentAI: *capability construction and internal power usage*.  - SignedAI: *trust, guarantees, and signatures*.  In FDIA/JITNA terms:  - Product layer is where:  - **I (Intent)** → user-facing UX flows,  - **D (Data)** → safely curated from Vault-1068,  - **Δ (Delta)** → becomes visible as product improvement,  - **A (Algorithm/Agent)** → manifests as assistants and tools,  - **R (Reflection)** → appears as verification & iteration,  - **M (Memory)** → persists via RCTDB and selectively via vault.  ---  ## 7.2 RCTLabs.co — Lab / Front Door & Floating Assistant  ### 7.2.1 Role of RCTLabs  RCTLabs is:  - the **public entry point** into the RCT ecosystem,  - a **lab-style environment** where new ideas and experiments can be showcased,  - the place where users first **feel** the difference between a generic LLM and a structured ecosystem.  [oai_citation:5‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)

Core responsibilities:  1. **Tell the Story**  - Why RCT exists (8 problems, context window crisis, verification, ecosystem thinking).  - How RCTLabs, ArtentAI, and SignedAI relate within one architecture.  - Why RCT is positioned as a **Thai-first infrastructure** (Chapter 8).  2. **Show the System in Action**  - Via the **Floating AI Assistant**, backed by the full architecture (Ch.2–6).  - Prefer **small, polished scenarios** over huge, unstable ones.  - Make the “ecosystem behavior” visible: routing, retrieval, verification.  3. **Collect Signals & Early Users**  - Visitors can:  - try curated flows,  - sign up for early access,  - provide feedback.  - Interactions are logged into **RCTDB** as:  - session records,  - experiment-like data points,  - future design signals.  ### 7.2.2 Floating AI Assistant — Concept & Architecture Link  The **Floating AI Assistant** is the main UX element on RCTLabs:  [oai_citation:6‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  - A “floating bubble” or panel that users can open on any page.  - Capable of:  - answering questions about RCT,  - running curated demo scenarios,  - showing multi-step reasoning (with safe, high-level traces).  From the user’s perspective, it feels like:  > “an AI that understands this ecosystem deeply,  > and can reason across architecture, experiments, and products,  > not just answer isolated questions.”  From the system’s perspective, a typical demo flow:  1. **Intent Parsing (Tier 1, JITNA)**  - Interpret user text as JITNA packet (I/D/Δ/A/R/M).  - Detect domain (RCTLabs / ArtentAI / SignedAI narrative).

<!-- Page 5 of 13 -->


 2. **Retrieval (Tier 2, Vault-1068 / GraphRAG)**  - Call `vault.search` / `graphrag.query` via MCP tools.  - Constrain to public-safe subset of vault documents.  3. **Planning (Tier 3)**  - Construct a response plan:  - explanation steps,  - diagrams to reference,  - when to call verification.  4. **Generation & Verification (Tiers 4–5)**  - Draft an answer, then run **SignedAI-lite checks** (e.g., basic consistency, spec alignment).  5. **Packaging (Tier 6)**  - Produce a clean message and, optionally, a simple reasoning trace.  6. **Memory & Reflection (Tiers 8–9)**  - Log the interaction to RCTDB (M, R).  - Mark issues or user signals for later improvement.  Thus, the Floating Assistant is not just UI — it is the **productized face of the 9-tier stack**.  ### 7.2.3 Public-Safe Use Cases on RCTLabs (Phase 1)  For Phase 1, RCTLabs focuses on **safe, bounded** scenarios:  [oai_citation:7‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  1. **Explain the RCT Ecosystem (Guided Q&A)**  - Prompts like:  - “Explain the RCT ecosystem like I’ma developer / researcher / founder.”  - Uses:  - curated vault docs,  - simplified diagrams,  - pre-approved narratives.  - Avoids direct exposure of:  - raw experiment logs,  - internal manifests (MANIFEST_1068),  - confidential algorithms.  2. **Architecture Walkthrough**  - “Show me how an RCT query flows through the 9 tiers.”  - Returns a structured step-by-step description with schematic diagrams (high-level).  - Maps UI to:

Think of ArtentAI as:

 > “a canvas + library system to compose high-level capabilities  > on top of the RCT architecture.”  Example capabilities:  - “Design and maintain a multi-LLM experiment campaign against 520 test cases.”  - “Generate and refine a 1,068-file vault from raw research inputs.”  - “Co-author and maintain whitepapers, docs, and spec trees with verification loops.”  ### 7.3.2 Internal Use Cases (Phase 1)  In early phases, ArtentAI is primarily used **by the Architect and close collaborators** to:  1. **Build and Maintain the RCT Ecosystem Itself**  - Manage:  - Vault-1068 structure,  - FDIA/JITNA tagging,  - manifest updates (MANIFEST_1068).  - Orchestrate:  - Windsurf-based coding sessions,  - integration of new MCP tools and containers.  2. **Design and Run High-Value Workflows**  - For example:  - RCT whitepaper v3 generation and maintenance,  - RCTLabs UX and narrative copy,  - packaging of benchmark summaries for internal/external use.  3. **Prototype Domain-Specific Assistants**  - e.g.,  - an “RCT Architect Assistant”,  - an “ArtentAI Capability Designer”,  - future vertical assistants for Thai SMEs, education, research labs.  ### 7.3.3 Relationship to FDIA & JITNA  ArtentAI makes FDIA/JITNA **explicit and configurable**:  [oai_citation:10‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  - Each capability/workflow can declare:  - its **primary FDIA emphasis** (e.g., “data-heavy”, “architecture-heavy”),  - its **JITNA profile** (e.g., I+Δ for planning, M+R for synthesis and review).

- Workflows can be annotated with:  - required tiers,  - verification requirements (SignedAI tier),  - memory integration strategy (how to write back to RCTDB and vault).  This allows:  - better routing at runtime (Tier 7),  - clearer expectations about which algorithms and tools will be involved,  - more interpretable logs and metrics for experiments.  In effect, ArtentAI is the **“design studio” for FDIA/JITNA-based workflows**.  ---  ## 7.4 SignedAI — Verification & Trust Layer  ### 7.4.1 Role of SignedAI  SignedAI is the **trust and verification product** in the ecosystem:  [oai_citation:11‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  - Oriented around:  - scoring,  - signing,  - and sometimes blocking outputs.  Its job is to ensure that:  > “Important outputs are not just generated; they are examined, evaluated,  > and signed by a combination of verification algorithms and human oversight.”  SignedAI serves:  - **internal needs** — verifying the RCT system itself,  - **external needs** — future customers who want verified outputs, configs, or workflows.  ### 7.4.2 Verification Tiers & UX  In product terms, SignedAI presents different “tiers” of verification, aligned with risk:  1. **Tier 0 — No Verification**  - Standard LLM-style response.  - Used only for low-risk or exploratory queries.


<!-- Page 9 of 13 -->


 2. **Tier 1 — Basic Consistency Checks**  - Checks:  - internal consistency,  - surface-level contradictions with known docs,  - formatting and structure.  3. **Tier 2 — Spec-Aware Verification**  - For outputs that must match:  - a spec in Vault-1068,  - expectations in RCTDB test cases.  4. **Tier 3 — High-Stakes Signed Verification**  - For:  - core system configurations,  - critical docs (e.g., pricing, policy),  - whitepaper releases.  - Includes:  - multi-pass verification,  - optional human review by the Architect,  - signed records in RCTDB.  In early phases, these tiers are mostly **internal**, but the concept is **productized** as SignedAI.  ### 7.4.3 Public-Safe Use Cases for SignedAI (Future-Facing)  Later, subsets of SignedAI can be exposed externally, for example:  - “Check this system design against RCT-style consistency rules.”  - “Score this AI workflow against SignedAI criteria for reliability.”  - “Generate and sign a simple spec for X with traceable reasoning.”  All public-facing SignedAI features:  - sit on top of the same Proof Layer (Chapter 5),  - use the same verification logs in RCTDB,  - but expose **limited, safe** entry points only. <!-- Page 10 of 13 -->


To understand how everything fits together, consider this **end-to-end flow**:  [oai_citation:13‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  1. **A user opens the Floating Assistant on RCTLabs**  - Triggers:  - gateway containers,  - orchestrator (Tier 7),  - initial logging in RCTDB.  2. **Orchestrator interprets the request via FDIA/JITNA**  - Uses:  - Tier 1 algorithms (intent parsing),  - JITNA roles to decide which tiers and tools to activate.  3. **Retrieval and Planning**  - Tier 2: retrieves from Vault-1068 / GraphRAG.  - Tier 3: plans steps and chooses which product/UX framing is appropriate.  4. **Algorithms Execute via the MCP Toolkit**  - LLM workers, retrieval tools, and specialized agents run inside the infra layer (Chapter 6).  - Intermediate artifacts and decisions are logged in RCTDB.  5. **SignedAI Verification (where configured)**  - Certain flows trigger Tier 5 verification.  - Verification results:  - are recorded in RCTDB,  - may influence routing (e.g., ask for revision, escalate to human).  6. **User Sees a Composed, Packaged Answer**  - Tier 6 packages the result in the correct product UX:  - RCTLabs (public narrative),  - ArtentAI (builder perspective, more detail),  - SignedAI (verification reports, scores).  7. **Proof & Memory Update**  - Experiment runners and batch jobs in ArtentAI can:  - aggregate results across many sessions,  - update experiments and delta records in RCTDB,  - feed insights back into Vault-1068 (curated docs, policies).

<!-- Page 11 of 13 -->


This loop is:  - **anchored in Vault-1068** for knowledge,  - **monitored by the Proof Layer** (Chapter 5),  - **powered by the infra/MCP layer** (Chapter 6),  - **exposed as products** in Chapter 7.  ---  ## 7.6 Public Narrative vs Confidential Reality  Because this is a **confidential internal chapter**, we can state explicitly:  [oai_citation:14‡Chapter07_Product_Line_and_Use_Cases.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d3447209bd07494c06778092)  - The **public narrative** (e.g., launched via RCTLabs) will:  - emphasize:  - “Thai-first AI infrastructure”,  - “ecosystem thinking vs single-model thinking”,  - “verification and memory as core design principles”.  - intentionally **hide** details about:  - exact test suites and measurements,  - precise container layouts and scaling strategies,  - internal vault structure and JITNA tagging,  - proprietary algorithms and heuristics.  - The **confidential reality** (this whitepaper) provides:  - a rigorous internal reference for:  - architecture,  - infra,  - proof layer,  - product relationships.  - a baseline for:  - future team members,  - investors or strategic partners (under NDA).  The product layer is therefore both:  - a way to **demonstrate** the ecosystem, and  - a **shield** that protects its deepest intellectual assets.  ---  ## 7.7 Summary and Bridge to Chapter 8
