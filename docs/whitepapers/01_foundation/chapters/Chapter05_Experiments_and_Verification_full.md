Where previous chapters covered:  - **Chapter 1** — The 8 AI Problems & Context Window Crisis  - **Chapter 2** — Ecosystem Architecture (3 domains, 9 tiers, multi-container design)  - **Chapter 3** — Vault-1068 & RCTDB as the knowledge and memory backbone  - **Chapter 4** — The 9-tier / 36-algorithm stack and meta-architecture  this chapter focuses on the **Proof Layer**:  - ~**520 test cases**  - accumulated into **15,000+ experiment runs** across models, configs, and versions  - logged and analyzed in **RCTDB**  - evaluated and scored via **SignedAI-style verification tiers**  [oai_citation:2‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  The goal is not merely to show benchmarks, but to:  > demonstrate that RCT is a *verifiable system*:  > its behavior can be tested, inspected, and improved systematically over time.  ---  ## 5.1 Goals of the Proof Layer  The Proof Layer serves four primary goals. It is the concrete expression of FDIA/JITNA in evaluation form.  ### 5.1.1 Validate Architectural Claims  Show, with data, that:  - the **9-tier, 36-algorithm stack** actually improves robustness and quality,  - **Vault-1068 + RCTDB** actually mitigate context-window limitations, rather than  just shifting complexity elsewhere.  [oai_citation:3‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  In FDIA terms:  - we measure whether the chosen **A (Architect/Architecture)**, applied over the  right **D (Data)** and **I (Intent)**, produces the desired **F (Future Outcome)**.  ### 5.1.2 Support Design Decisions  Provide evidence for:  - choosing certain algorithms over others within tiers,

<!-- Page 3 of 14 -->


- prioritizing particular containers and infra designs,  - deciding when multi-LLM or additional verification is worth the extra cost  and latency.  This allows the Architect (Phase 0) to treat design choices as **hypotheses** that can be **tested and compared**, not as irreversible commitments.  ### 5.1.3 Enable Safe Evolution  Every change to:  - algorithms and prompts,  - routing strategies and tier configurations,  - memory structures (Vault-1068 organization, RCTDB schema, JITNA tagging),  should be testable against a **baseline suite** of experiments.  This enables:  - **regression detection** — quickly seeing if a change breaks behaviour elsewhere,  - **comparative evaluation** — choosing between multiple candidate designs,  backed by objective and subjective metrics.  ### 5.1.4 Build Trust & Transparency  - **Internally (Architect, collaborators, future team members)**  - Confidence that the system behaves as expected under realistic load.  - Traceable reasons behind major design decisions.  - **Externally (future public-facing docs)**  - High-level, non-sensitive benchmark narratives that can be shared.  - A clear story: *how* RCT was tested, not just headline scores.  ---  ## 5.2 Design of the Test Suite: ~520 Test Cases  The RCT test suite is organized into **~520 test cases**, grouped into several categories.  [oai_citation:4‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  Each test case is:  - defined as a **specification** in Vault-1068 (human-readable description, intent, risk class),  - stored as a **structured test definition** in RCTDB (input schema, scoring method, linked FDIA/JITNA roles),  - attached to one or more **tiers/algorithms** and product contexts (RCTLabs / ArtentAI / SignedAI).

 ### 5.2.1 Context & Retrieval Tests  Designed to stress:  - long-context behaviour,  - retrieval accuracy from Vault-1068,  - recall of specific details across multiple files and snapshots.  Examples:  - “Can the system correctly answer questions that require combining 3–5 documents?”  - “Does it resist forgetting earlier constraints late in a conversation?”  [oai_citation:5‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  These tests are where the **Context Window Crisis** (Chapter 1) is attacked directly:  - comparing naive long-context prompting vs FDIA/JITNA-aware retrieval + Vault-1068 + RCTDB.  ### 5.2.2 Algorithm & Tier-Specific Tests  Aim to test:  - individual tiers (1–9),  - specific algorithm families (routing, planning, verification, synthesis, etc.).  Examples:  - **Tier 1** — intent parsing accuracy across noisy or ambiguous input.  - **Tier 3** — decomposition quality and delta planning for complex tasks.  - **Tier 5** — ability to detect contradictions vs specs in Vault-1068.  [oai_citation:6‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  These tests make it possible to ask:  - “Is failure due to retrieval, planning, generation, or verification?”  ### 5.2.3 Multi-LLM & Routing Tests  Evaluate:  - when to call large vs small models,  - how well routing decisions follow policies and cost/latency constraints.  Examples:

- cost-limited scenarios (e.g., “fit within X tokens / Y USD”),  - latency-limited scenarios,  - high-risk vs low-risk task classes, where heavy verification must or must not be used.  These tests are closely tied to **Tier 7 (Orchestration & Routing)** and help tune routing policies.  ### 5.2.4 Memory & Delta Tests  Focus on:  - **RCTDB integration**,  - applying **Δ (deltas)** over time,  - maintaining long-term consistency across sessions and projects.  Examples:  - “Does the system remember prior decisions for a project after N steps?”  - “Does it avoid re-introducing rejected patterns or deprecated designs?”  [oai_citation:7‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  These tests operationalize **Δ, R, M** in JITNA:  - Δ — what changed?  - R — what did we learn?  - M — what do we remember and reuse?  ### 5.2.5 Product & UX-Oriented Tests  Simulate:  - **RCTLabs** Floating Assistant flows (user-facing demo scenarios),  - **ArtentAI** capability chains (intent → workflow → artifact),  - **SignedAI** verification paths for high-stakes outputs.  Measure:  - correctness and stability,  - clarity of explanations,  - UX robustness across mobile/desktop contexts.  ---  ## 5.3 15,000+ Experiments — From Single Runs to Systemic Patterns  The **15,000+ experiments** are *not* 15,000 different test cases. They are:


<!-- Page 6 of 14 -->


- repeated runs of the ~520 test cases,  - across:  - multiple models,  - configuration variants (prompting, routing, constraints),  - time (before/after architectural changes, container changes, vault restructurings).  [oai_citation:8‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  ### 5.3.1 Variation Dimensions  Examples of variation dimensions include:  1. **Model Backend**  - Different LLMs with:  - varying context lengths,  - reasoning strength,  - cost profiles.  2. **Routing Policy**  - Aggressive vs conservative routes:  - more/less reliance on Tier 5 verification,  - different sets of tiers activated or skipped.  3. **Memory Strategy**  - With/without:  - certain vault subsets,  - specific RCTDB memories,  - delta integration strategies.  4. **Safety & Constraints**  - Tighter vs looser constraints from Architect codex,  - different SignedAI thresholds and decision rules.  ### 5.3.2 What the Experiments Look For  The goal is to uncover **systemic patterns**, such as:  - which combinations of tiers + models reduce failure modes most effectively,  - when context-based improvements plateau (more context yields diminishing returns),  - which verification strategies give the best trade-offs between quality, latency, and cost.  [oai_citation:9‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)

 These patterns are then written back into Vault-1068 as:  - design updates,  - routing policies,  - codex rules,  - and guidance for future product decisions.  ---  ## 5.4 Benchmark Methodology in RCT  Benchmarking in RCT follows a few guiding principles that differentiate it from purely model-centric benchmarks.  ### 5.4.1 Task-First, Not Model-First  - Tests are defined as **tasks the ecosystem must solve** (e.g., “correctly merge two specs without losing constraints”),  - not as “run this model on X dataset and measure accuracy”.  Models are part of the solution, but **not the center of the evaluation**.  ### 5.4.2 FDIA / JITNA-Aware Metrics  Metrics are aligned with FDIA/JITNA roles. For example:  [oai_citation:10‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  - **I-quality** — Did the system correctly interpret and preserve intent?  - **D-coverage** — Did it use the right data from Vault-1068/RCTDB?  - **Δ-effectiveness** — Did it achieve the required change in state or artifact?  - **A-appropriateness** — Was the chosen architecture/tier stack proportional to risk/complexity?  - **R-depth** — Did reflection catch obvious issues and edge cases?  - **M-reusability** — Can the result be safely stored and reused as memory?  These metrics turn FDIA/JITNA from **design language** into **evaluation language**.  ### 5.4.3 Multi-Stage Evaluation  Many tests are evaluated at several points:  - after **Tier 4 (Draft Generation)**,  - after **Tier 5 (Self-Critique & Verification)**,  - after **Tier 6 (Synthesis & Packaging)**.  [oai_citation:11‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)

<!-- Page 8 of 14 -->


 This makes it possible to:  - locate where failures tend to appear,  - see how much improvement each tier adds,  - decide whether to invest more computation in later tiers.  ### 5.4.4 SignedAI as Externalized Verifier  Instead of trusting the same model that generated outputs, RCT introduces **specialized verification tiers** (SignedAI-style) to **separate generation and judgment**.  [oai_citation:12‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  This reduces:  - self-confirmation loops,  - blind spots where the same model overlooks its own failures.  ---  ## 5.5 SignedAI and the Verification Tiers  **SignedAI** is both:  - a **conceptual layer** (verification-first thinking), and  - a **product direction** (a named service for trust and evaluation).  ### 5.5.1 Defining Verification Axes  SignedAI helps define verification axes such as:  - correctness vs spec,  - factual accuracy,  - safety and risk level,  - reproducibility and determinism,  - compliance with Architect codex.  [oai_citation:13‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  Each axis may have:  - scoring rubrics,  - thresholds,  - guidance for “gray zone” decisions.  ### 5.5.2 Evaluating Outputs

<!-- Page 9 of 14 -->


SignedAI uses dedicated algorithms (often with different models/tools from the generator) to:  - re-check facts against Vault-1068 and, when allowed, external sources,  - compare outputs with test specs in RCTDB,  - assess consistency across multiple runs and configurations.  ### 5.5.3 Signing, Revising, or Blocking  For high-stakes outputs, SignedAI may:  - **sign** an output as passing (with metadata and explanation),  - request revision (loop back via Tier 4/5),  - **block** the output entirely if risk is too high.  [oai_citation:14‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  In all cases, the decision is **logged**.  ### 5.5.4 Feeding RCTDB  All verification decisions are logged in RCTDB, including:  - scores and axis-level details,  - justifications and commentary,  - linked test cases and configurations.  From a FDIA/JITNA perspective:  - **F** — ensures future outcomes are acceptable,  - **D** — produces new structured data points,  - **Δ** — documents remaining gaps and changes,  - **R** — captures reflection,  - **M** — adds to long-term records.  SignedAI is thus a **central actor** at the Proof Layer, not an afterthought.  ---  ## 5.6 Experiment Pipeline and RCTDB Integration  Every experiment is treated as a **first-class citizen** in the system.  [oai_citation:15‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  ### 5.6.1 Definition Stage (Vault & RCTDB)  A test case is:

<!-- Page 10 of 14 -->


- described in **human-readable form** in Vault-1068 (problem, intent, risk, expected behavior),  - structured as a **test record in RCTDB**, including:  - input specification,  - expected behavior or acceptable ranges,  - scoring method,  - relevant FDIA/JITNA roles,  - links to related docs and tiers.  ### 5.6.2 Execution Stage  A runner:  - sends inputs via RCTLabs / ArtentAI / SignedAI gateways,  - triggers the **full tiered stack (1–9)** where appropriate.  Runs can be:  - fully automated,  - semi-automated with human-in-the-loop at certain tiers,  - interactive (e.g., Floating Assistant test scripts).  ### 5.6.3 Collection Stage  RCTDB stores:  - raw inputs,  - intermediate outputs (per tier, when feasible),  - final outputs,  - verification results (SignedAI),  - runtime metrics (latency, cost, model choices, container mapping).  [oai_citation:16‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  ### 5.6.4 Analysis Stage  Analysis agents and humans:  - aggregate results across runs,  - detect patterns (e.g., systematic weaknesses per domain/tier),  - produce **synthesis docs** in Vault-1068:  - experiment summaries,  - post-mortems,  - recommendations.  ### 5.6.5 Integration Stage  Insights feed back into:


<!-- Page 11 of 14 -->


- algorithm refinements and prompt changes,  - routing policies (Tier 7),  - vault restructuring (e.g., better FDIA/JITNA tagging),  - codex updates (Architect decisions).  This forms a **closed loop**:  > Vault → Algorithms → Experiments → RCTDB → Synthesis → Vault (updated) → …  The Proof Layer is therefore not separate from the rest of RCT;  it is a **built-in nervous system** for feedback and adaptation.  ---  ## 5.7 How Experiments Connect Back to the Context Window Crisis  The Context Window Crisis (Chapter 1) highlighted:  [oai_citation:17‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  1. **Volume** — too much information to fit in a single window.  2. **Relevance** — hard to choose what to include.  3. **Temporal Drift** — models struggle with evolving knowledge.  The experiment suite explicitly tests:  - whether **Vault-1068** and **FDIA/JITNA-aware retrieval**:  - reduce hallucination vs long-context baselines,  - improve selection of relevant documents,  - maintain consistency across multi-step workflows.  - whether **RCTDB + Δ/R/M roles**:  - allow the system to adapt over time without regression,  - successfully recall prior decisions in long workflows,  - expose drift and misalignment early.  Many experiments are designed as **A/B/C comparisons**:  - **A:** Single-model, long-context baseline.  - **B:** Multi-tier RCT without RCTDB integration.  - **C:** Full RCT (Vault-1068 + RCTDB + verification).  This enables:  - quantitative comparison (error rates, stability, latency, cost),  - qualitative comparison (quality of justification, traceability, narrative clarity).

---  ## 5.8 Limits, Biases, and Future Experiment Directions (Confidential)  Because this is an **internal, confidential chapter**, we can state limitations directly.  [oai_citation:18‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  ### 5.8.1 Test Suite Coverage  - 520 test cases is large relative to manual curation,  but still small relative to the full state space of tasks the ecosystem may face.  - Coverage is strongest in:  - AI tooling, infra, documentation, UX,  - technical reasoning.  - Coverage is weaker in:  - open-domain world knowledge,  - value-laden, highly subjective judgment tasks.  ### 5.8.2 Model Evolution  As models evolve:  - some findings may become outdated,  - new failure modes may appear.  RCTDB is designed to track this (versioned runs, model IDs, container maps),  but it requires **continuous upkeep** and periodic re-running of core tests.  ### 5.8.3 Verification Bias  SignedAI’s criteria are:  - constructed by the Architect and collaborators,  - refined over time via experiments and reflection.  This encodes a specific **value system** — powerful, but not “neutral”.  Awareness of this bias is part of the design: the system is **explicitly opinionated**.  ### 5.8.4 Infrastructure Constraints  Some experiments are shaped by:  - hardware limits,  - budget constraints,  - time and operational capacity.  Not all theoretically desirable experiments are feasible at once.


<!-- Page 13 of 14 -->


The Proof Layer prioritizes:  - high-leverage tests,  - core safety and correctness,  - systemically informative experiments.  ### 5.8.5 Future Directions  Planned directions include:  - broadening domain coverage (non-technical and cross-cultural tasks),  - increasing automation in generating and triaging new test cases,  - integrating selected external public benchmarks where appropriate,  - preparing a **public, privacy-safe subset** of experiments for external release.  ---  ## 5.9 Summary and Bridge to Chapter 6  In this chapter, we have:  [oai_citation:19‡Chapter05_Experiments_and_Verification.pdf.pdf](sediment://file_00000000d53072099c9306ec0e31330a)  1.
